Travel Photo Page

by Yi Chen

 

 

 
 

This fall locates almost at the bottom of the canyon, and I cannot catch up my breath after I climb back to our car. A high-quality zoom will save you energy and time for exchanging lens. A ND filter is also a must to shot water. I used the smallest aperture to obtain longer shutter speed in this photo. A polarizer also helped for longer exposure.
 
This photo was taken on August 22, 2001, the last day of our summer trip, before the President View of Mt. Rushmore. I set up my tripod and intent to photograph those flags and the pattern of this building, but the old gentleman passing by added a lot to this photo.
 
Lots of people stopped their cars by the road to take photos of the wild animals. I was using EF100mm/f2.0 USM, the longest focal lens I have, to get the closest look. I whistled to him, to make him looked up at my camera. I regret that I should use fill flash in this photo. Hehe, the guy behind the tree is my roommate ;-)
 

You cannot say you have been to Yellowstone National Park if you have seen Old Faithful Geyser. It erupts every 76 minutes, and it was named for its consistent performance by members of the Washburn Expedition in 1870.

 
 

Tower falls in great canyon of Yellowstone. We visited the fall in the last day in Yellowstone.

 

Petrified Tree, an excellent example of an ancient redwood.
 

 

Mt Rushmore is a huge project, lasted more then twenty years to finish the President View. There are a couple of huge stones at the foot of the mountain. You will see two presidents from the whole. The built-in fill-flash in Canon Elan 7E contributed the light for the stones, and that is one of the reason I haven't switch to Nikon system yet.
 

I still haven't get the name of those dogs. They looks very cute: slim necks, slim legs. They don't walk, they jump! One of my friend told me they are racing dogs. When shooting dogs or other pets, don't forget to switch your AF to "Al Servo"!

 

People will gather together around the Old Faithful Geyser according to the estimation of "show time", but we just waited twenty more minutes for this show.

 

A global view of Old Faithful Geyser before its show time.
 

A photographer was taking photograph of the fall, from the artist point of Yellowstone. This point gives an excellent overview of the tower falls, grand canyon and Yellowstone river. Lots of artist and photographers stay here for their work.
