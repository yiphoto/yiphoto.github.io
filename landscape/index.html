Landscape & Cityscape

 
 
A friend of mine take me to Kansas City in the end of March. This photo is my first landscape photo after I bought my first SLR, Canon Elan 7E. It was taken at a rest area by the inter-state highway. I was using the 28mm end of the EF28-135mm/f3.5-5.6 USM.
 

 

Lincoln is a very small and quite city, and I don't have much chance to photograph landscapes. But we has a couple of very beautiful lakes in the city, and it is my first subject to learn landscape photography. May is the start of the summer, and I only have about two minutes to catch the sunset when it is touching down the horizon. So within 3 days I calculate the exact time when the sun touch down, and go back the second day to shot again. This is also my first try to use slide film. I shot the sunset with a whole 36-frame roll of Fujichrome Sensia II 100, in two days. Most of them were under-exposured, since I metered at the sun, not the sky. This would be a good subject to learn light metering technique.
 
Actually I shot photos for a group of students and instructors in the ITA workshop in the summer of 2001, but I need to finish the other 34 frames so that they can get the prints as soon as possible. All I have done was grabbed my gears and finished that roll with night photos. Esther L. Kauffman Academic Residential Center is opened in the spring of 2001. I was shotting the Nebraska Union from the door of the Kauffman Center. Nebraska Union is the student activity center. I plan to take another photo of the Kauffman Center some day.
